# 1. How the Model Works

The Fractional Standard pricing model is an allocation-based retainer with role-differentiated pricing and fixed monthly retainers. Clients purchase a tier of dedicated Operator allocation — a defined level of time and capacity from the Operator — and pay a fixed monthly fee for that allocation. They see a tier name, an allocation level (expressed in approximate weekly or monthly hours for clarity), and a fixed monthly price. They never see hourly rates, are not required to approve Operator hours, and the Retainer is not time-and-materials billing.

The shift from a "client buys hours" framing to a "client buys allocation" framing is intentional and material. It means:

- Clients buy access to capacity, not a metered hour count
- Operator hours flex within a month based on Client needs, Operator availability, and project rhythm
- Operator hours are tracked internally in Clockify for capacity management, margin accounting, and tier-fit purposes only
- Client receives no operator timesheets and is not asked to approve hours
- Sustained scope expansion beyond the tier allocation is addressed through a managed conversation (Chris or Kevin → Client), not through hourly invoicing

## 1.1 The Three Layers

**Layer 1 — Client-Facing:** The client sees a tier name (Foundation, Growth, Scale, Embedded), an approximate level of effort (e.g., "~10 hours/week of fractional COO time"), and a fixed monthly retainer. No hourly rate is disclosed. The framing is access at a defined capacity level.

**Layer 2 — Operator-Facing:** The operator names an internal hourly rate during onboarding; Kevin checks it against one threshold number per role. The hourly rate is used to derive a fixed monthly fee per engagement (calculated as weekly hours target × hourly rate × 52/12, rounded to the nearest $25). Operators log hours weekly in Clockify as a utilization signal only — hours are not the basis for pay. Operators are paid on the 15th of each month for the prior month’s services. Twelve payments per year per engagement.

**Layer 3 — Internal (Company):** Fractional Standard retains the spread between the client retainer and the operator's fixed monthly fee. Every engagement is priced to target 40% net margin at full allocation utilization. Any underutilization is pure margin upside, retained by Fractional Standard. Operator hours data is internal only.

# 3. Operator Compensation

Operators are paid a fixed monthly fee per engagement, calculated from their internal hourly rate × the engagement's tier weekly hours target × 52/12 (rounded to the nearest $25). There are no bands or tiers on the operator side — a single hourly rate per role drives the fee calculation. During onboarding, the operator names their rate. Kevin checks it against one threshold number per role. If the rate fits, the operator is approved. If it doesn't, it's a conversation, not an automatic rejection. Operator hours are logged weekly in Clockify as a utilization signal for capacity management, margin accounting, and tier-fit purposes — hours are not the basis for pay, and clients are not required to approve, sign off on, or audit Operator hours.

The target operator is not a seasoned Fortune 500 CXO. It is a senior manager or director who has been close to the executive seat at a startup or mid-tier company and wants to go independent. # 5. Margin Protection Mechanisms

Five structural mechanisms protect margin without requiring adversarial billing conversations.

### 5.1 Full-Allocation Pricing Rule

Every retainer is priced assuming the operator logs 100% of the tier allocation hours. This means the stated margin is the worst case. If an operator on a Growth engagement (~40 hours/month allocation) logs 32 hours/month on average, the actual net margin on that engagement is higher than the stated 44%. This is by design: conservative pricing with built-in upside.

### 5.2 The Rate Check (One Number)

Kevin maintains one threshold number per role. When an operator names their rate, Kevin checks it against the threshold. If it fits, proceed. If it doesn't, it's a conversation about whether the rate works for both sides. There are no bands, no negotiation matrices, no sliding scales.

### 5.3 Scope Expansion Conversation (MSA Section 6.4)

If client engagement scope sustainably expands beyond what the current Tier allocation supports — observed via internal hours utilization data — Chris or Kevin initiates a conversation with the client. Options: tier upgrade, supplemental engagement (project-based), or scope reduction. The Retainer is a fixed fee; clients are not separately invoiced for time worked above the tier allocation in any single month. Sustained patterns (two or more consecutive months) trigger the conversation.

### 5.4 Role-Based Pricing (No Cross-Subsidy)

Each role's pricing reflects its own operator cost structure. COO placements are priced higher because COO operators cost more. If COO margins tighten, the response is adjusting COO pricing — not subsidizing from CFO revenue. This keeps the margin architecture clean.

### 5.5 The 7% Burden Buffer

The 7% burden rate covers insurance, payroll taxes, platform costs, and administrative overhead, applied as 7% of operator cost (not revenue). This is baked into every margin calculation, not treated as an afterthought. If actual burden comes in under 7%, the excess flows directly to net margin.

# 6. FTE Conversion Fee Structure

Fractional engagements may convert to direct hire (FTE Conversion). The conversion fee is governed by MSA Section 9 and Exhibit D and structured as a tenure-based tier:

| **Tenure at Conversion** | **Conversion Fee** | **Notes** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0–3 months | **20% of FY1 Base Salary** | Plus 3-Month Minimum Buyout if Tenure < 3 months |
| 3–6 months | **17% of FY1 Base Salary** | No buyout |
| 6–12 months | **13% of FY1 Base Salary** | Reduced tier |
| 12+ months | **10% of FY1 Base Salary** | Lowest tier |

**3-Month Minimum Buyout (MSA Exhibit D.2.1):** If Conversion occurs before the Operator has completed 3 full calendar months of Tenure, Client pays an additional Buyout calculated as Monthly Retainer × (3 − Tenure in months). No Buyout if Tenure ≥ 3 months.

**12-Month Tail Period (MSA Exhibit D.2.2):** If Client (or any affiliate) engages a previously-placed Operator within 12 months after the engagement ends — for any reason — the conversion fee still applies, with Tenure measured from the original engagement start date.

**No Replacement Guarantee for FTE Conversions:** Operator was observed during the fractional engagement, so conversion is caveat emptor. Fractional Standard works with clients in good faith on unexpected separations but is not contractually obligated.

**FY1 Base Salary** excludes bonuses, commissions, and benefits, but includes equity granted within the first 12 months at grant-date fair market value.

# 9. Billing Cadence (Settled)

**Settled: Monthly client billing, monthly operator pay (calculated on quad-normalized basis).**

## 9.1 Client Billing — Monthly (12 Periods/Year)

Fractional Standard bills clients monthly in advance. Each monthly invoice is issued on or about the Engagement Start Date anniversary of each calendar month, payable net-14 via Stripe with a pre-authorized payment method on file. Twelve invoices per year per engagement; the per-invoice amount matches the stated monthly retainer (e.g., a CFO Growth client pays $11,850 every month).

The decision to bill monthly (rather than on a 4-week / 13-period quad cycle) was made for client clarity: monthly retainer figures are intuitive ("we pay $11,850/month") and align with how clients budget and book operating expenses. The 8.3% annual revenue uplift available under quad-at-same-per-period pricing was rejected as opaque relative to clean monthly framing.

## 9.2 Operator Pay — Monthly Fixed Fee (Quad-Normalized)

Operators are paid on the 15th of each month for the prior month's services. Twelve payments per year per engagement.

Operator compensation is a **fixed monthly fee per engagement**, calculated as:

`Monthly Fee = (Weekly Hours Target × Internal Hourly Rate × 52) ÷ 12`, rounded to the nearest $25.

This formula normalizes the 4-week ("quad") work rhythm into a monthly pay cycle. The operator receives the same fixed monthly amount regardless of whether a calendar month contains 4.0 or 4.4 work-weeks. Hours are logged weekly in Clockify as a utilization signal — they are not the basis for pay.

**Why this structure:** Pure monthly hours-based pay would create operator income variance every month (calendar months containing five Mondays vs. four). Pure quad-based pay would produce 13 payments per year, mismatched against 12 client invoices. The chosen structure — monthly cadence, quad-normalized math — gives operators predictable income that matches the client billing rhythm and eliminates working-capital float pressure (client cash arrives by mid-month; operator pay goes out on the 15th).
